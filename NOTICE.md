# Art and licensing notes

## This project's art is original

Every component graphic in Breadboard Builder (the breadboard, the LED, the
resistor, the wires, the Arduino) is hand drawn SVG written for this project.
None of it is copied from another library. That means the art carries no
third-party license obligations and you can do whatever you want with it.

## If you ever want higher-fidelity art

Two well known open sources of realistic electronics part art exist. They were
researched and their licenses verified against the actual license files before
this note was written. The summary matters because "open source" does not mean
"free to lift the art."

### Wokwi elements — recommended if you borrow

- Package: `@wokwi/elements` (repo: `github.com/wokwi/wokwi-elements`).
- License: **MIT** (Copyright (c) 2020 Uri Shaked). Verified against the repo's
  LICENSE file.
- What it is: 40+ realistic, inline-SVG parts (arduino-uno/mega/nano, esp32,
  breadboard, led, resistor, pushbutton, potentiometer, 7-segment, LCD, sensors).
  Presentation only, no simulation logic.
- Obligation: just preserve the MIT copyright notice. Commercial use is fine.
- Catch: the art lives inside Lit web components, and some parts (the breadboard,
  the resistor's value-driven bands) are generated by code, so you cannot paste
  the TypeScript source and get usable SVG. The clean path is a one-time
  extraction: load the component, render it once in a browser, serialize the
  resulting SVG, and inline that static string. Do not ship the whole Lit bundle.

### Fritzing parts — avoid lifting these

- Repo: `github.com/fritzing/fritzing-parts`.
- License of the **part graphics and .fzp metadata**: **CC-BY-SA 3.0**
  (attribution + share-alike). Verified against `fritzing-parts/LICENSE.txt`.
- Important split: the Fritzing **application** is GPLv3, but that does not
  govern the art. Do not conflate the two.
- Two real obstacles:
  1. **Share-alike** is viral on the artwork: any derived Fritzing SVG you
     redistribute must itself be released under CC-BY-SA 3.0.
  2. Fritzing's **own FAQ** states they do not allow use of their part graphics
     "in other software systems at the moment" and asks you to contact them for
     an exception. That request cuts against reusing the art even though the CC
     license, once granted, cannot be revoked for already-released files.
- Net: the part SVGs are technically trivial to extract (plain files at
  `svg/core/breadboard/NAME_breadboard.svg`), but reusing them is a goodwill and
  possible trademark risk and drags share-alike onto your assets. For a tool you
  may publish, prefer original art (what this project does) or Wokwi's MIT art.
